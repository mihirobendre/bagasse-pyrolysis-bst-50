What is heat duty?

The rate of energy that must be supplied to the reactor to maintain it at the target outlet temperature. Higher temperature = more energy input required to sustain pyrolysis.

Why does it increase with temperature?

At higher outlet temperatures, more energy must be supplied to heat the products and sustain the pyrolysis reactions. The reactor demands progressively more heat input as the target temperature rises.

ZamGrow design point

At 600–700°C, the reactor releases ~1,700–1,850 kW per kg/s. This heat is recovered via syngas recycling to keep the kiln self-sufficient.
